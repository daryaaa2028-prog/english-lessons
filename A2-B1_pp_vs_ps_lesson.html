Grammar · Quick Revision

The Difference at a Glance

You already know these tenses — here's a quick reminder of the key contrast.

✦ Present Perfect

have / has + past participle
  • Life experiences (no time given)
  • Something that still affects now
  • Unfinished time period
"I have visited 12 countries."
"She has already eaten."
ever never already yet just recently this year so far

✦ Past Simple

verb + -ed / irregular form
  • Finished event at a specific time
  • Series of completed actions
  • When the time is known
"I visited France in 2018."
"She ate at 7 o'clock."
yesterday last year in 2005 ago when I was… in the morning
Key rule to remember:
Use Present Perfect when the exact time is not important or not mentioned.
Use Past Simple when you say when something happened — or it's clearly finished.

A Life Well Travelled

Maria is 52 years old and lives in Barcelona. She has travelled to over 30 countries in her life. She first left Spain when she was 24 — she went to Argentina for six months and worked there as a language teacher.

Since then, she has returned to South America four times. Last summer, she visited Colombia and climbed the famous Cocora Valley. It was the hardest thing she has ever done physically, but she says it completely changed her perspective on life.

Maria has just booked a trip to Japan for next spring. She has never been to Asia before, and she is very excited. "I have always wanted to see cherry blossoms," she says.
