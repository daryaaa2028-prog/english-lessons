🗣 Speaking 2
Hobbies Interview
9B · Hobbies · like/enjoy/love/hate
Take turns asking and answering. The teacher asks — you answer. Then you ask your teacher the same question. Use full sentences with love/like/enjoy/don't like/hate + -ing.
💬 Useful structures
I love + -ing I really enjoy + -ing I like + -ing I don't like + -ing I hate + -ing Do you like + -ing? What do you enjoy doing? Me too! / Really? Not me.
1
Do you like cooking?
Follow up: "What do you like cooking?" / "Who taught you?"
2
What do you enjoy doing at the weekend?
Follow up: "Do you do it alone or with friends?"
3
Do you enjoy watching films or reading more?
Follow up: "What kind of films / books do you like?"
4
Is there a hobby you hate? Why?
Use: "I hate + -ing because…"
5
What hobby would you like to start? Why?
Use: "I'd like to try + -ing because…"
✅ Self-check — did you…
Teacher note: Model the target structure before each question if needed. Check for -ing form after the verb. Encourage the student to elaborate beyond one-word answers.

🗣 Speaking 3
Role-play: Plan Your Weekend
9D · Making Suggestions
You and your teacher are friends planning the weekend together. Create a short dialogue — make suggestions, agree, disagree, and agree on a plan. Aim for 6–8 turns each.
📋 Your scenario
It's Friday afternoon. You and a friend want to do something fun this weekend. You want to go outside (a park, a market, a walk). Your friend prefers something indoors (a café, a film, cooking at home). Make suggestions and find an activity you both like!
💬 Phrases you must use (tick when used)
Why don't we…? Let's… How about…? That's a great idea! I'm not sure. OK, sounds good!
Start the conversation
Make the first suggestion using "Why don't we…?"
e.g. "Why don't we go to the park on Saturday?"
Disagree politely
When your teacher suggests something you don't like, use "I'm not sure…"
e.g. "I'm not sure. I don't really like…"
Make a compromise
Suggest something that works for both of you using "How about…?"
e.g. "How about going to the market and then a café?"
Agree on the plan
End the conversation with an agreement and a detail (time / place).
e.g. "OK, sounds good! Let's meet at 11."
✅ Self-check — did you…
Teacher note: Prompt with "But I don't like…" to force a new suggestion if the student runs out of ideas. Correct errors after the role-play rather than mid-task. Check for all 6 suggestion phrases. Can extend by swapping roles.

Writing: Using because
9C · Skills · Writing
Write 5 sentences about your study habits. Use because in each sentence.
Model sentences

I study English in the morning because I am more focused then.
I don't use my phone when I study because it's a distraction.

  • 1. When do you study? (morning / evening / at the weekend…)
  • 2. Do you like studying alone or with others?
  • 3. What do you use to help you learn? (app, notebook, flashcards…)
  • 4. How long do you study each day?
  • 5. Why do you want to learn English?
✏️ Teacher note: Review together. Check: correct use of because, sentence structure, and verb forms.

🗣 Speaking 4
General Conversation
All topics · Free speaking
The teacher asks — you answer naturally. No preparation needed. Try to give more than one sentence for each answer. It's OK to ask the teacher questions too!
1
Tell me about your family. Who do you live with?
2
Who is your favourite person in your family? Why?
3
What do you like doing in your free time?
4
Is there a hobby you want to try? Why?
5
Why do you study English? How long have you been learning?
6
When and where do you usually study? Do you have a routine?
7
What are your plans for the weekend? What do you want to do?
8
Do you like taking photos? Who or what do you like to photograph?
9
What is one thing you love doing and one thing you hate doing? Why?
10
If you could suggest one activity for this weekend, what would it be?
Teacher note: Use these freely — pick the questions that feel most natural given the conversation. Follow up on interesting answers. Note any recurring errors from the lesson for a final error-correction slot. This is a chance to hear the student use all Unit 9 language in an unprompted context.
