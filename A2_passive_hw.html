📚 Grammar Rules — The Passive Voice
Present Simple Passive
Subject + am / is / are + past participle
→ Emojis are used all over the world. (every day, always, general facts)
Past Simple Passive
Subject + was / were + past participle
→ The first emoji was created in Japan in 1999. (completed past action)
Present Perfect Passive
Subject + has / have + been + past participle
→ This song has been downloaded over a billion times. (at some point up to now)
💡 We use the passive when we don't know who did something, or when the action is more important than the person who did it.
To say who did the action, add by + agent: "The film was directed by Greta Gerwig."
