Present Simple Passive  ·  Past Simple Passive  ·  Present Perfect Passive

📖 Grammar Rules
Tense Formula Example
Present Simple Passive am / is / are + past participle English is spoken here.
Past Simple Passive was / were + past participle The cake was eaten yesterday.
Present Perfect Passive have / has + been + past participle My keys have been lost.
💡 Tip: Use the passive when you don't know who did the action, or when it's not important.
To choose is/are or was/were, look at the subject and the time: singular subject → is / was; plural subject → are / were.
